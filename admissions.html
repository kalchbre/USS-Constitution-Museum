Admissions

The USS Constitution is owned by the United States Navy and operated separately from the Museum. Visit the USS Constitution's website for more information on visiting the Ship

Museum Admission

The Museum has suggested admission tiers of:

  • Pay it Forward: $25 per person
  • Standard: $15 per person
  • Reduced: Free – $10 per person

Please help us keep our doors open for all by being as generous as possible. The USS Constitution Museum is proud to offer several discounted or free admission programs to individuals, families, and groups. Please see our reduced pricing below:

Ways to Save
  • Museum Members: Free admission and 10% discount in the Museum's Store
  • Military: Free admission to all active and retired military members with photo I.D. From Armed Forces Day to Labor Day, admission is free for active-duty military families thanks to the Museum’s participation in Blue Star Museums.
  • EBT, WIC and ConnectorCare Cardholders: We are proud to participate in the Card to Culture program and offer free admission to EBT cardholders and their immediate family/guests. See the full list of participating organizations offering EBT, WIC, and ConnectorCare discounts.
    The Card to Culture program is a collaboration between Mass Cultural Council and the Department of Transitional Assistance, the Women, Infants & Children (WIC) Nutrition Program, and the Massachusetts Health Connector, by breaking down financial barriers to cultural programming.
  • Library Pass Program: Free admission for up to nine people with a valid pass from a participating Massachusetts public library. Check your local library to see if they are a participating member and for availability of the Library Pass.
  • Massachusetts’ Teachers: Free admission for teachers with a valid I.D. badge.
  • Museum Professionals: Free admission for volunteers and staff with presentation of staff card.

Museum Policies

All visitors must check in at the Admission desk prior to entry into the Museum exhibits. The safety of our visitors and staff remains our top priority. We ask all visitors to:

  • Wash or Sanitize hands frequently
  • Do not visit if they are experiencing any symptoms of COVID-19
  • Do not eat or drink in the exhibits

Ship Admission

The Ship is free to visit on a first come, first serve basis. Please be aware that visitors must pass through a security inspection and show federal or state photo identification, such as a driver’s license or passport, before embarking on USS Constitution. Visitors under the age of 18 do not require a photo identification card.

Ship Security and Policies

All visitors to USS Constitution are required to pass through a security screening, and show federal or state photo identification, such as a driver’s license or passport. Visitors under the age of 18 do not require a photo identification card, but are required to go through the screening process. Similar to airport security, visitors will be asked to remove bulky articles of clothing such as jackets, all jewelry, watches, belts, and all bags, luggage, or backpacks to allow the item to be examined. Visitors must keep all personal belongings with them at all times while touring the ship. Strollers are not allowed aboard USS Constitution, and must be left on the pier while touring the ship. No chewing gum or open food or beverage containers are allowed aboard the USS Constitution unless they can be sealed (i.e. water bottles with caps).
